# Triple Bottom Line (TBL) Scoring Rubric
## Comprehensive Guide for Rating Grocery Brands

---

## Overview

This rubric converts multiple data sources into a unified 0-10 scoring system for Social, Environmental, and Economic dimensions of the Triple Bottom Line framework.

---

## Data Sources Available

### 1. **B Corp Certification** (Primary Social & Environmental Indicator)
- **Source**: https://www.bcorporation.net/en-us/find-a-b-corp
- **Data**: B Impact Assessment scores (0-200 points, 80+ = certified)
- **Coverage**: 9,576+ certified companies globally
- **Categories Assessed**:
  - Governance
  - Workers
  - Community
  - Environment
  - Customers

### 2. **Open Food Facts Eco-Score** (Environmental Data)
- **API**: https://world.openfoodfacts.org/api/v0/product/{barcode}.json
- **Data Available**:
  - `ecoscore_grade`: A to E (environmental impact)
  - `nutriscore_grade`: A to E (nutritional quality)
  - `nova_group`: 1-4 (food processing level)
  - Product category, ingredients, packaging
- **Coverage**: 3.5+ million products

### 3. **ESG Ratings Providers** (Corporate-level ratings)
- **MSCI ESG Ratings**: AAA to CCC (https://www.msci.com/esg-ratings)
- **Sustainalytics ESG Risk**: 0-100 (lower = better)
- **Note**: Most require paid subscriptions, limited free access

### 4. **Specific Certifications** (Binary: Yes/No)
- B Corp Certified
- Fair Trade Certified
- Carbon Neutral/Carbon Trust
- Leaping Bunny (Cruelty-Free)
- Cradle to Cradle
- Green Seal
- USDA Organic
- Rainforest Alliance

## Scoring Formula

### **SOCIAL SCORE (0-10)**

#### Base Components:
1. **Labor & Workers Rights** (0-4 points)
   - B Corp Worker Score: Convert 0-200 → 0-4 (score ÷ 50)
   - Fair Trade Certification: +1 point
   - Living Wage Commitment: +1 point
   - Worker Safety Record: +1 point
   - Diversity & Inclusion Initiatives: +0.5 points

2. **Community Impact** (0-3 points)
   - B Corp Community Score: Convert 0-200 → 0-2 (score ÷ 100)
   - Charitable Giving/1% Pledge: +0.5 points
   - Local Sourcing: +0.5 points

3. **Supply Chain Ethics** (0-3 points)
   - Supply chain transparency: +1 point
   - Anti-child labor policies: +1 point
   - Ethical sourcing certifications: +1 point

**Conversion Table for Non-B Corps:**
- If no B Corp data, use manual research:
  - Excellent labor practices = 8-10
  - Good practices = 6-7.9
  - Average/compliance only = 4-5.9
  - Poor/controversy = 0-3.9

---

### **ENVIRONMENTAL SCORE (0-10)**

#### Base Components:
1. **Carbon & Climate** (0-4 points)
   - Carbon Neutral Certified: +2 points
   - Published carbon reduction targets: +1 point
   - Renewable energy usage (>50%): +1 point
   - Eco-Score A: +2, B: +1.5, C: +1, D: +0.5, E: 0

2. **Packaging & Waste** (0-3 points)
   - Recyclable/compostable packaging: +1 point
   - Reduced plastic commitment: +1 point
   - Cradle to Cradle Certified: +1 point

3. **Sustainable Sourcing** (0-3 points)
   - USDA Organic: +1 point
   - Rainforest Alliance: +0.5 points
   - Sustainable agriculture practices: +1 point
   - Water conservation efforts: +0.5 points

**Open Food Facts Integration:**
- Eco-Score A = 9-10 environmental score
- Eco-Score B = 7-8.9
- Eco-Score C = 5-6.9
- Eco-Score D = 3-4.9
- Eco-Score E = 0-2.9

---

### **ECONOMIC SCORE (0-10)**

#### Base Components:
1. **Financial Stability & Growth** (0-4 points)
   - Publicly traded/financial transparency: +2 points
   - Sustainable revenue growth: +1 point
   - Long-term viability (10+ years): +1 point

2. **Fair Pricing & Value** (0-3 points)
   - Competitive pricing: +1 point
   - Value for quality: +1 point
   - No price gouging history: +1 point

3. **Economic Impact** (0-3 points)
   - B Corp Economic Score: Convert 0-200 → 0-2
   - Job creation/retention: +0.5 points
   - Support for local economies: +0.5 points

**Note**: Economic is often the hardest to assess without corporate financials. Default to 5.0 (neutral) unless clear positive/negative indicators exist.

## Grade Calculation

**Overall TBL Score** = (Social + Environmental + Economic) ÷ 3

**Grade Assignment:**
- **EXCELLENT**: 8.5 - 10.0
- **GREAT**: 7.0 - 8.4
- **GOOD**: 5.0 - 6.9
- **POOR**: 0 - 4.9

---

## Step-by-Step Scoring Process

### For Each Brand:

1. **Check B Corp Status**
   - Search: https://www.bcorporation.net/en-us/find-a-b-corp
   - If certified, extract Impact Assessment score
   - Apply certification-based scoring

2. **Check Product-Level Environmental Data**
   - Use Open Food Facts API for brand's products
   - Average Eco-Scores across products
   - Apply environmental formula

3. **Check Specific Certifications**
   - Fair Trade: https://www.fairtrade.net
   - Carbon Trust: https://www.carbontrust.com
   - Leaping Bunny: https://www.leapingbunny.org
   - Add certification bonuses

4. **Research Controversies**
   - Google: "[brand name] controversy labor environmental"
   - Deduct points for major issues:
     - Labor violations: -2 Social
     - Environmental disasters: -2 Environmental
     - Financial fraud: -2 Economic

5. **Calculate Final Scores**
   - Sum all components
   - Cap at 10.0 per dimension
   - Calculate overall average & grade

## Quick Reference Examples

### **Patagonia** (B Corp, #92.5 Impact Score)
- **Social**: 9.5 (B Corp worker score 45/50, Fair Trade +1.5, activism +1)
- **Environmental**: 9.8 (B Corp env score 48/50, Carbon Neutral +2, Organic +0.5)
- **Economic**: 8.5 (Stable growth, fair pricing, transparency)
- **Overall**: 9.27 → **EXCELLENT**

### **Coca-Cola** (No B Corp)
- **Social**: 3.5 (controversies in water rights, labor issues in supply chain)
- **Environmental**: 3.0 (plastic pollution, water depletion, no carbon neutral)
- **Economic**: 6.0 (financially stable, but extractive practices)
- **Overall**: 4.17 → **POOR**

### **Brand with No Data**
- **Default to 5.0, 5.0, 5.0** (neutral/unknown)
- Indicate "UNKNOWN" grade
- Update as research is completed

## Data Update Schedule

- **B Corp certifications**: Update quarterly (companies recertify every 3 years)
- **Open Food Facts**: Real-time API, updates continuously
- **Specific certifications**: Annual verification recommended
- **Controversies**: Monitor news monthly for major brands

## Implementation Notes

1. Start with brands you know well (Patagonia, Nestlé, etc.)
2. Use B Corp scores as your "gold standard" calibration
3. For non-B Corps, compare to similar B Corp scores
4. Be consistent: document your research process
5. Update scores annually or when major news breaks
6. Consider adding a "Confidence" rating (High/Medium/Low)
